## Core Frameworks

### 1. The Value Equation

The single most important framework Hormozi teaches. Value is NOT just "what you deliver" -- it's a ratio:

```
         Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value = ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
         Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice
```

**To increase value, do any combination of:**
- Increase the Dream Outcome (make the result bigger, more desirable)
- Increase Perceived Likelihood (reduce risk, add proof, offer guarantees)
- Decrease Time Delay (deliver results faster)
- Decrease Effort & Sacrifice (make it easier for the customer)

**Why this matters:** Most people try to increase value by lowering price. That's a race to the bottom. Instead, increase the numerator (outcome + likelihood) and decrease the denominator (time + effort). This lets you RAISE prices while making the offer more compelling.

**AI Prompt Pattern:**
> "Let's run your offer through Hormozi's Value Equation. What's the dream outcome? How likely do people believe they'll achieve it? How fast? How much effort on their part? Where's the biggest lever to increase value?"

### 2. The Grand Slam Offer

An offer so compelling that people feel stupid saying no. Not because it's cheap -- because the VALUE is overwhelming.

**Components:**
1. **Identify the Dream Outcome:** What does the customer really want? Not your product -- the transformation
2. **List Every Problem:** What obstacles stand between them and the outcome? List ALL of them
3. **Turn Problems into Solutions:** For each obstacle, create a solution. These become your offer components
4. **Assign Delivery Methods:** For each solution, choose the highest-value, lowest-cost delivery method
5. **Stack the Value:** Add bonuses, guarantees, urgency, scarcity

**The Problem-Solution List:**
| Customer Problem | Your Solution | Delivery Vehicle |
|-----------------|---------------|------------------|
| "I don't know where to start" | Step-by-step onboarding | Automated sequence |
| "What if it doesn't work?" | 30-day money-back guarantee | Policy |
| "I don't have time" | Done-for-you setup | Service team |
| "I've tried before and failed" | Success stories + accountability | Community |

**Pricing Rule:** Price based on the value of the outcome, not the cost of delivery. If your product helps someone make $100K more per year, charging $10K is a bargain.

### 3. The $100M Offers Framework (Offer Creation Process)

**Step 1: Pick Your Market**
- Massive pain (they're desperate to solve it)
- Purchasing power (they can afford a solution)
- Easy to target (you can find and reach them)
- Growing market (trend is your friend)

**Step 2: Charge Premium Prices**
- "There is no strategic benefit to being the second cheapest" -- Hormozi
- High prices attract better clients who get better results
- High prices fund better delivery which creates better testimonials
- High prices create a "premium perception" that increases perceived value

**Step 3: Create Scarcity & Urgency**
- Limit capacity (real, not fake)
- Seasonal offers with genuine deadlines
- Cohort-based programs (doors close)
- Price increases that reward early action

### 4. The Lead Generation Framework ($100M Leads)

Four ways to get customers, in order of control:

**Warm Outreach** (free, slow)
- Reach out to people who already know you
- Personal messages, not mass blasts
- "I'm working on [X]. Know anyone who struggles with [Y]?"

**Content** (free, slow, compounds)
- Give away your best ideas for free
- Hook → Retain → Reward pattern
- Volume matters: 100 pieces of content before judging what works

**Cold Outreach** (free, fast, doesn't scale forever)
- Personalized messages to strangers
- Lead with value, not a pitch
- "I noticed [specific thing about them]. I made [specific thing for them]..."

**Paid Ads** (costs money, fast, scales)
- Only after you've validated the offer organically
- Target look-alikes of your best customers
- Creative matters more than targeting in 2024+

**The Scaling Sequence:**
1. Validate offer with warm outreach (0-10 customers)
2. Prove repeatable with cold outreach (10-50 customers)
3. Amplify with content (build authority)
4. Scale with paid ads (proven offer + proven audience)

### 5. The CLOSER Framework (Sales)

**C** - Clarify why they're there
**L** - Label their problem with authority
**O** - Overview their past experiences (what they've tried, why it failed)
**S** - Sell the vacation, not the plane ride (outcome, not process)
**E** - Explain away their concerns
**R** - Reinforce the decision

**Key Insight:** People don't buy products. They buy the gap between where they are and where they want to be. Your job is to make that gap feel urgent and your solution feel inevitable.

## Key Principles

### The "Niche Down" Principle
The riches are in the niches. Don't sell to "businesses" -- sell to "B2B SaaS companies with 10-50 employees struggling with churn." The narrower the niche:
- The more specific your messaging
- The higher the relevance
- The easier to become the authority
- The less competition
- The higher the prices you can charge

### The Goodwill Cycle
Give > Ask > Deliver > Repeat. Build a massive imbalance of goodwill before asking for anything. This applies to content, networking, partnerships, everything.

### The "1000 True Fans" Model (Kevin Kelly via Hormozi)
You don't need millions of customers. You need 1,000 people who each pay you $1,000/year = $1M business. Or 100 people at $10,000/year. Or 10 people at $100,000/year.

### Speed of Implementation
The #1 predictor of success: how fast you implement after learning. Not how much you know -- how quickly you act on it. In a thinking session, this means: leave with ONE action you'll take today.

## Example AI Prompts

### Phase 0 (User Stories)
- "Forget the product for a moment. What transformation does your customer want? What's the before and after? Paint me the picture of 'after.'"
- "If your customer achieved the dream outcome, what would their life/business look like? That 'after' state is what you're really selling."

### Phase 1 (MINE)
- "Let's run the Value Equation. Dream outcome: [X]. Perceived likelihood right now? Time to results? Effort required from the customer? Which of those four levers can you move the most?"
- "What are ALL the problems standing between your customer and the dream outcome? List every single one. Don't filter -- we'll organize later."

### Phase 3 (ASSAY)
- "For each option, let's build a Grand Slam Offer. What problems does it solve? What's the stack? What would make someone feel stupid saying no?"
- "Are you pricing based on cost of delivery or value of outcome? If the outcome is worth $100K to your customer, what should you charge?"

### Phase 4 (CRUCIBLE)
- "What's your guarantee? If you can't guarantee something, it means you don't believe in your own product enough. What CAN you guarantee?"
- "Would your best customer refer 3 friends right now? If not, the product isn't good enough yet. What would need to change?"

### Phase 6 (PLAN)
- "What's your first 10 customers plan? Not 10,000 -- 10. Who are they specifically? How will you reach them this week?"
- "What's one action from this plan you can take in the next 24 hours? Speed of implementation is the #1 predictor of success."
